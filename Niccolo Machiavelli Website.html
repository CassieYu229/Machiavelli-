Biography

Niccolò Machiavelli was an Italian Renaissance political philosopher and writer, born in Florence. He served as a diplomat and secretary to the Florentine Republic.

Major Works

  • The Prince - His most famous work on political power
  • Discourses on Livy - Analysis of republican government
  • Art of War - Military strategy treatise

Legacy

Machiavelli's ideas fundamentally shaped modern political thought and continue to influence leaders, strategists, and political scientists worldwide.
